# Glossary

This glossary explains acronyms and technical terms used in the project.

## Institutional acronyms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| BCRD | Central Bank of the Dominican Republic. Key source for macroeconomic, labor, monetary, and price data. |
| ONE | National Statistics Office. Key source for official statistics, surveys, and socioeconomic data. |
| ENCFT | Continuous National Labor Force Survey. Used to measure employment, informality, labor income, and other labor-market indicators. |
| TSS | Social Security Treasury. Relevant for registered formal workers and contributors. |
| DIGEPRES | General Budget Directorate. Source for budget execution and public spending. |
| DGII | Internal Revenue Directorate. Source for tax revenue and tax-based economic activity context. |
| MEPyD | Ministry of Economy, Planning and Development. Source for macro, social, and development context. |
| MITUR | Ministry of Tourism. Source for tourism context. |
| SB | Superintendency of Banks. Source for credit, banking, and financial stress context. |

## Economic terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Selective squeeze | A situation where economic pressure does not affect everyone equally. Some groups may be cushioned while others remain exposed. |
| Macro resilience | Broad signs of strength, such as activity, external inflows, employment, or tourism. It does not prove comfort across all households. |
| Formal channel | Workers captured by formal records, such as social-security contributors. It does not automatically represent informal workers. |
| Informal worker | A worker outside full formal labor registration or protection. |
| Cushioning | The ability of income, subsidy, or support to soften price or spending pressure. |
| Wage-food offset | A comparison between wage movement and food-price pressure to test whether a group could absorb part of the shock. |
| Household trade-down | Changes in buying behavior: cheaper brands, smaller quantities, smaller units, lower purchase frequency, or basket changes. |
| Trade-down | Switching toward cheaper or smaller options. In this project, direct current 2026 trade-down is not claimed as proven. |
| Basket | A group of goods used to measure cost of living, consumption, or prices. |
| CPI | Consumer Price Index. Measures price changes for a basket of goods and services. |
| MIPYME | Micro, small, and medium-sized enterprise. Relevant for financing stress, credit costs, and business activity. |
| Remittances | Money sent from abroad to households. Aggregate remittances do not automatically prove comfort across all households. |
| Fiscal context | Data on public spending, transfers, subsidies, debt, or budget execution. |

## Method terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| NOT_PROVEN | Remains unproven. It does not mean false; it means the available evidence is not enough to claim it. |
| REJECTED_BOUNDARY | Rejected inference boundary. A conclusion cannot validly be drawn from that evidence. |
| Context only | Useful for understanding the environment, but insufficient to prove a main claim. |
| Evidence bridge | Data needed to connect a pressure signal to a conclusion. For example, trade-down requires direct behavior evidence. |
| Period alignment | Evidence must match the time period of the claim. 2025 evidence does not automatically prove a 2026 claim. |
| Unit alignment | The measured unit must match the claim. Employment is not income; price is not quantity purchased. |
| Coverage alignment | The population covered by the data must match the population of the claim. Formal workers are not all households. |
| Red-team | An exercise that attacks the thesis from rival positions to test whether it survives without overclaiming. |
| Key public bibliography | Curated list of main sources for public reading. |
| Local audit registry | Broader technical registry kept locally, not necessarily published fully because of size or noise. |

## Final line

**Not collapse. Not comfort. A selective squeeze.**
