Technical terms

Compact definitions of acronyms, indicators, and methodological boundaries used in the publication.

BCRD

Central Bank of the Dominican Republic. Main source for macroeconomic, tourism, general inflation, food inflation, and CPI-item data used in the visuals.

Source

CPI

Consumer Price Index. Used here to compare general inflation, food inflation, and specific items such as garlic, green bananas, and fresh chicken.

Indicator

TSS

Dominican Social Security Treasury. Useful for the formal-worker channel, but it does not automatically represent informal workers or all households.

Formal channel

MITUR

Ministry of Tourism. Institutional source or context for tourism statistics, arrivals, tourism activity, and cross-validation where applicable.

Source

ONE

National Statistics Office. Dominican statistical source used for household, labor, distributional, and structural context.

Source

ENCFT

Continuous National Labor Force Survey. Useful for employment and labor-market context; not equivalent to household income if income/remuneration tables are missing.

Survey

Tourism arrivals

Foreign air-arrival count used as a tourism-volume indicator. It helps explain macro stability, but does not prove household comfort.

Tourism

Tourism monetary inflow

Estimated tourism spending or income, shown here in millions of U.S. dollars. It is a macro stabilizer, not direct proof of household welfare.

Tourism

Food inflation

Food-price variation. It explains daily pressure, but does not by itself prove that households changed purchasing behavior.

Prices

Food basket items

Specific goods such as garlic, green bananas, or fresh chicken. They make price pressure concrete through everyday products.

Prices

Formal channel

Evidence applying to registered or formal contributors. Its boundary is that it must not be generalized to informality or whole households.

Boundary

Informality

Workers or income outside the formal TSS channel. Informal-worker offset remains unproven with the current data.

Open bridge

Offset

Compensation between income growth and price pressure. It can be supported for one channel without being proven for all households.

Concept

Whole household

The full family or domestic unit, potentially with mixed income sources. It cannot be inferred from the individual formal channel.

Unit

Trade-down

Switching toward cheaper products, brands, quantities, or unit sizes. In this investigation, direct current trade-down remains unproven.

Behavior

Causal bridge

Evidence needed to connect observed pressure with real behavior or relief. Without the bridge, the claim remains unproven.

Method

Context only

Evidence that helps interpret the environment but is insufficient to prove a stronger claim.

Status

Not proven

Status for claims that may be true or false but were not demonstrated by the reviewed sources.

Status

Rejected boundary

An inference the project does not allow, such as converting formal-worker data into a conclusion about all households.

Status

Year-over-year change

Comparison with the same period of the previous year. Used to show 2025 → 2026 changes when the source reports annual growth.

Method

Derived base

A 2025 value mathematically reconstructed from a 2026 value and its year-over-year rate when the public row does not show both values together.

Method

Endpoint/API

Technical access point used for discovery or reconstruction. It is not presented as a normal reader-facing source if it is not browser-readable.

Technical
This glossary explains terms used in the publication; it does not add new claims or change the evidence boundaries.
